The Rise of Acidic Coffee

Acidic coffee, often referred to as "coffee with acidity," has been a long-standing trend in many foreign countries. Over time, this trend found its way into South Korea, and it has taken the coffee scene by storm. You might be wondering, why has acidic coffee become so popular? Let's take a closer look at the factors behind this phenomenon.

Defining Coffee Quality through Acidity

One of the key reasons behind the popularity of acidic coffee lies in its impact on coffee quality. Coffee with good acidity showcases a diverse range of flavors, offering a cleaner and more nuanced taste experience. As coffee quality improves, the acidity level becomes a crucial factor in defining the overall taste profile of the coffee.

The Connection with Coffee Bean Quality

The rise of acidity in coffee is intricately linked to the improvement in coffee bean quality. Coffee farms have been dedicatedly working to produce beans with excellent acidity, resulting in an influx of high-quality coffee beans in the market. The competition among coffee farms to produce the best beans has led to a natural progression towards better coffee quality.

The Journey of Coffee Beans from Farm to Cup

The journey of coffee beans from farm to cup involves various steps that contribute to the enhancement of acidity in coffee. With the improved quality of beans, coffee farms have gained recognition and support, leading to better working conditions and welfare for the laborers. This transformation has not only improved the quality of coffee but also the livelihood of those involved in the coffee farming industry.

Preserving the Intrinsic Taste through Light Roasting

We, at Coffee Bin, believe in preserving the intrinsic taste and aroma of coffee beans. Light roasting is our chosen method to achieve this goal. Just like roasting pork belly to enjoy its true taste, we carefully roast coffee beans to bring out the best flavors without compromising on taste.

The Role of Coffee Equipment and Baristas

However, making light roast coffee that perfectly preserves the flavors is not without its challenges. Coffee equipment, such as the espresso machine, has seen significant developments to extract the full flavor from robust light roasts. Skilled baristas play a crucial role in understanding and utilizing the features of these machines to bring out the best in each cup of coffee.
